What we look for

We care about a small number of things, deeply.

We do not have a checklist. These are the qualities that consistently predict success in the lab.

  • Clear thinking about language and knowledge. You are drawn to questions about how models represent meaning, how they fail to reason, and what it would actually mean to fix that.
  • Strong engineering judgment. You can build experiments that are fast, correct, and readable. You know when to cut scope and when not to.
  • Research taste. You have a sense of what makes a question interesting and what makes a result meaningful — and you are willing to defend it.
  • Comfort with uncertainty. Early-stage labs require people who can work in conditions where the right direction is not yet obvious.
  • (Helpful but not required) Prior exposure to NLP, ML, or systems programming. We are happy to train people whose foundations are strong.

How to apply

Getting in touch.

We read every message. We respond faster when the email makes it easy to understand what you want to work on and why.

By email

Send a short introduction to breathai@ntu.edu.sg

Subject line: [PhD 2026], [RA Application], or [Visiting — Your Institution].

Include: a CV, a brief paragraph on what you want to work on and why BreathAI, and links to prior work (code, preprints, project pages, GitHub). One page is enough.

For PhD applicants: also submit a formal application through the NTU SCSE PhD admissions portal, and mention BreathAI in your research statement.
