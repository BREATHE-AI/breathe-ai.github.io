Research

Built around four questions.

Our work sits at the intersection of language model reasoning, agentic systems, rigorous evaluation, and multilingual NLP. We care about research that holds up outside the paper setting.

Research Areas

What we work on

A

Language Reasoning

Training, adapting, and evaluating models for compositional reasoning, multi-step planning, and reliable decision-making. We study how language models acquire reasoning capabilities, where systematic failures occur, and how to make model outputs verifiable.

Chain-of-thought · Multi-hop QA · Planning · Verifiable inference

B

Language Agents

Designing and evaluating agents that use tools, write and execute code, retrieve structured knowledge, and coordinate with other agents to complete complex, open-ended tasks. We focus on reliability, interpretability, and graceful failure recovery.

Tool use · Code generation · Multi-agent · Agentic evaluation

C

Evaluation and Alignment

Building benchmarks, auditing methods, and safety-oriented frameworks that reveal model behavior at fine granularity, well before deployment. We prioritize evaluations that are hard to game and that surface the failure modes practitioners actually care about.

Behavioral auditing · Fine-grained benchmarks · Safety · Human annotation

D

Multilingual NLP

Extending language technology to underserved communities through cross-lingual transfer, low-resource language modeling, and grounded multilingual datasets. We pay particular attention to Southeast Asian languages and to evaluation that goes beyond translation.

Cross-lingual transfer · Low-resource · SEA languages · Multilingual benchmarks
